26th August 2023: Holy Cross College, Nagercoil

The comprehensive one-day entrepreneurship workshop for women: Science for Sustainable Livelihood was conducted at Holy Cross College Nagercoil, and was sponsored by Emcure Pharmaceuticals as part of its CSR initiative.

The workshop was conceived to empower young women between the age-groups of 18 to 25, for encouraging skill development, entrepreneurial spirit, and subsequently achieving sustainable economic independence. About a hundred young women from neighbouring villages were engaged in technical sessions by resource persons for creating entrepreneurial spirit and giving them knowledge on available governmental and non-governmental funding resources and subsidised schemes for women entrepreneurs for starting a business.

V. Samuel Gnana Prakash, EMBO Fellow, DAAD Fellow & Marie Curie Fellow, illustrated the indispensable role of science in magnifying entrepreneurial opportunities, particularly emphasizing its significance in the regional context.

Arul Jerald Prakash, a renowned scientist & former Director of Kerala State Science and Technology Museum & Priyadarshini Planetarium, Trivandrum, delved deep into the realm of eco-entrepreneurship. He championed the idea of women utilizing local resources, tapping into government schemes, and thriving as entrepreneurs, especially in an era of limited job opportunities.

Focused Hands-on Training and Workshop Sessions were conducted in four distinctive areas:

Fish Product Preservation & Marketing: This session provided hands-on training in Fish products manufacturing, Meat and Vegetable pickle making and gave insights into the potential of the culinary sector, emphasizing nuances, product packaging, and the importance of FSSI certification.

Jute Product Creation & Sales: Led by Ms. Packialakshmi from M/s Sarakontrai Jute Products, participants created mini jute items like jute purses, carry bags and files instilling practical skills for potential business ventures using the sewing machines and jute material given to each of them.

Vermicomposting & Vermiculture: Attendees were introduced to sustainable farming practices, specifically the art of vermicomposting at the Cross Verm Composting Centre. They were provided with organic grow-bags, cow-dung, earth-worms, dry leaves and

accessory equipment using which they made miniature composting bins. Each bin was expected to make 3 to 4 kg of vermicompost. This practical hands-on would help them to scale up in a larger level of business.

Aquarium Production & Fish-keeping: The Multipurpose Hall turned into an aquatic entrepreneurial hub, with in-depth hands on training on making aquarium tanks followed by fish breeding sessions and discussions on fish breeding techniques, aquarium design, aerator pumps, decorative materials for aquarium followed by business mentoring for the same. The participants were provided with glass pieces, silicone gel, shot glue guns and other material required for the training.

At the end of the event, certificates were distributed to all participants.
