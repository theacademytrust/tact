20-23 July 2024 in partnership with Kutuhali, Parivarthana Ranga Samaja, Arivu Trust and Kalasuruchi Mysuru, played to house-full audiences composed of students and the general public. Day 1: Vignana Vikasada Vaada, a Kannada play on scientific temperament down the ages, from Galileo to Darwin to the present. Directed by Yatheesh Kollegala and performed by Viveka Ranga, a troupe of girl students hailing from more than half-adozen districts across the state.

Day 2: AC vs DC, a Kannada play by faculty members of an Engineering College in Dharwad, on the conflict between Edison and Tesla, and its societal ramifications during their times. The play held up a mirror to professionalism and ethics (or their lack thereof) among scientists by taking the example of one of the longest-running scientific disputes.

Day 3: Galileo's Daughter, an English play by Parivarthana troupe composed of exISRO scientists, theatre veterans and first-timers, was a gripping performance of the timeless story of a scientist's struggle against unyielding authority and irrational society.

Day 4: Hasire Honnu, a Kannada biographical play by Arivu group, on eminent Kannada litterateur BGL Swamy and his substantial contributions to science, specifically in the field of Botany.

Apart from the plays, each day featured a prelude event: Dialogue excerpts from science plays in English and Kannada, science-themed songs in Kannada, and an Art-&Mathematics exhibition curated by BS Krishnamurthy.

The event was widely covered by the press, and received glowing reviews in both traditional as well as social media.
