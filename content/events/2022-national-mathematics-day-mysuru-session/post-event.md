22nd December 2022: tAcT collaborated with the Ramanujan Mathematics Club and the Department of Mathematics of Acharya Patshala College to celebrate National Mathematics Day 2022. More than 100 participants from different colleges attended the event which sought to enhance students' mathematical reasoning and problem-solving abilities, with the long-tern view of inspiring students to pursue careers in mathematical sciences and research. Two sessions were held during the program.

V. Sivashankara Sastry, veteran math communicator, made a presentation on "Magic Squares in Indian Culture" by discussing their historical and philosophical significance, their early presence in temples, their application in tantric traditions, and the advanced mathematical methods for their construction outlined by ancient Indian mathematicians like Nārāyaṇa in his work Gaṇitakaumudī. B.S. Krishnamurthy, Founder of Ganitha Kunitha, Mysuru, held a session on "Dance of Maths - The Art of Learning Mathematics".
