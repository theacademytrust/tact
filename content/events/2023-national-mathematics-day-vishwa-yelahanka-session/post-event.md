Ganitha Mela 2023, 18 - 22 December 2023 (Monday to Friday), held at Vishwa Vidyapeeth, Yelahanka Organized jointly by tAcT, Vishwa Vidyapeeth School, Seed2Sapling Education; and supported by Karnataka State Council for Science and Technology, the week-long event witnessed the convergence of over 8000 math enthusiasts, students, and teachers from more than 60 schools from not only Karnataka but other states.

The event was inaugurated by Padma Shri Kiran Kumar, the renowned space scientist associated with ISRO. His words set the tone for the days to come, emphasizing the role of observations, asking questions and significance of mathematics in our lives.

The participants engaged in Harates led by several mathematicians and scientists like Kiran Kumar of ISRO, Sujatha Ramdorai of University of British Columbia, Rohini Godbole, Tirthankar Bhattacharyya, Phoolan Prasad, MRN Murthy and Utpal Nath of IISc, Amitabh Joshi of JNCASR, Rajaram Nityananda, Samuel Joseph and Supurna Sinha of ICTS, and Nithin Nagaraj of NIAS.

Other popular science and maths communicators like Kollegala Sharma, VSS Sastry and BS Krishnamurthy also conducted harates and activity sessions.

Ganitha Mela offered a plethora of math-related activities, puzzles and games that catered to participants of all ages.

The interactive activity stalls became hubs of excitement, where students and enthusiasts delved into the world of numbers, patterns, and logic.

The visitors experienced several activities around math concepts like pi, Pythagoras theorem, Fibonacci sequence, etc. in the form of games and puzzles.

Classical games like Aaliguli Mane, Snakes and Ladders, Tower of Hanoi and many others added to the entertainment component.

The evenings at Ganitha Mela 2023 were a celebration of the cultural aspect of mathematics.

From classical and folk music to dance performances, and the acclaimed kannada play "Lilavathi" on the life of Bhaskara II, the cultural programs showcased the intersection of mathematics and the arts.

These included shadow puppetry by Padma Shri Parshuram Gangavane, a classical dance Jugalbandhi of Bharatanatyam and Kathak, a musical jugalbandi of Carnatic classical instruments, a show on geometry in soap foams and bubbles by Samuel Joseph and Supurna Sinha, and other math-based performances like math-magic and mindreading.

Maths-related activity kits were distributed to all the visiting students, and all speakers and guests were honoured with custom-made Ganitha Mela mementos.

The Ganitha Mela was widely reported as a first-of-its-kind event in several newspapers, TV channels and social media platforms.
